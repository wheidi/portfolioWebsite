Revitalizing a low-use dashboard

Role

Product Designer

Type

Full-Time

Tools

Figma, Notion, Figjam

Team

1 Product Designer (me!)

Impact

+20% page usage

The problem

Our platform dashboard had a significantly low usage rate 📉.


As a core page to the platform experience, how can we increase the usefullness of the dashboard?

The process

Discovery Research Design Prototype Outcomes
Research

Discovering the problem

Looking into user heatmap data, I found a great disparity on one of our core pages.


91%

Most users that visited the dashboard did not interact with it

#1

The dashboard was the most viewed page on our platform


The core action on the dashboard is clicks, however only 9% of users that visited the page clicked on anything. Being such a high-traffic page, and a page in our onboarding, the dashboard could greatly impact a majority of our user experiences and should be improved.

Defining a target audience

Through user surveys, interviews, and usage recordings, I gained a picture of a user's motivations with the dashboard.

The problem with our current experience

I reviewed our current experience against the feedback from our users.

Insights

  • Crucial items were located under the fold of a typical desktop laptop, the most common device for our users
  • Low relevance information at the top of the page, especially for returning users
  • Important items such as upcoming appointments or tasks were missing

Defining Goals

How might we help students determine their next course of action?


Our users are busy and navigate a complex space of university admissions, scholarships, and other activities. We should give them clarity on what they need to do next to achieve their goals.

Goal metrics to monitor

  • Increase click-through on the dashboard
  • Time on page does not increase significantly

Ideating Solutions

I reviewed dashboards from education software, project management software, and productivity software.

Insights

  • Users should be able to follow up on any item displayed to them
  • Overcrowding a dashboard can lead to feeling overwhelmed
  • Information must be shown in context. Users want to know what a number is now, and also how it changed from before

Designing the progress bar

The question

    How can we accurately keep track of progress, what's to come, and capitalize on the feeling of progress?

Key design outcomes

  • 3 separate loading bars to represent 3 separate sections
  • The current section's loading bar is maximized
  • User is more likely to view the progress bar when it will be growing - when the user interacts with the “Next” button

Addressing general privacy worries

The question

    How can we address the right privacy worries at the right time?

Key design outcomes

  • We address general privacy worries at the beginning, so users proceed with this knowledge in mind

Addressing specific privacy worries

The question

    What if users are worried about their privacy while filling out the form?

Key design outcomes

  • An extra info button beside sensitive questions to address specific worries about how or why this information will be used

Impact

Profiles with collected data

Challenge: Designing for everyone

How could we design a profile builder that felt inclusive and usable for everyone? This was one of the major challenges of this project. Making the questions non-ambiguous, the answer options inclusive, and thinking about the connotations of visual indicators across different cultures. This is just a small set of the many different things I learned about when designing with inclusivity in mind. A challenging but necessary goal to have!



Looking forward: Progress bar potential

I believe a profile builder has a lot of opportunities to be fun and exciting. Moving forward, I’d love to design a similar profile builder that draws on the elements of character building in many games. In other words, how could we gamify a profile builder?

This could be done simply through the progress indicator. Making a lightbulb light up, a flower bloom, or a slime blob grow into a personalized slime. The possibilities are endless!
