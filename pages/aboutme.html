🧑🏾‍🤝‍🧑🏼 Psychology: Understanding others and myself

"Positive emotions encourage approach-behaviour". Psychology can tell us so much about how users perceive experiences; how emotions and styles of motivation can encourage or discourage behaviours. Incorporating these principles into my design process and work habits provide me great insight into a user's experience and my own work patterns as well.

🧢 Empathy: Wearing different hats

A big part of understanding an audience is empathizing with them. This includes the user, the industry, and any identity or community involved. Whenever I encountered a question where there was less research to inform the next step, I found that a solid starting point would almost always be to put on my user hat, become the user, and go from there.

🎇 Awe: A sense of wonder

A more personal preference, but I found that a good, smooth experience always gave me some sense of awe. Research here also points to awe being affiliated with a slew of positive psychological effects that I won't get into, but that we can definitely chat about 😉. Even something as simple as a loading animation can elicit awe, and thus is an experience that I personally aspire to craft.
