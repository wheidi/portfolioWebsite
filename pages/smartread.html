What is Fake News?

We’re exposed to an abundance of news articles, written pieces, and other information sources on social media. How do we know the validity of these sources - are they backed by research, or spreading misinformation?

Presenting the credibility score

Testing Insights

  • A lot of information at once, users feel reluctant to look through it all - it feels like "work"
  • Users are confused as to how these scores are calculated, “why is something 74% vs 70%?”

Final iteration

Key Design Decisions

  • Implemented gradual disclosure in the form of drop down menus to remove more detailed, less relevant information from the screen
  • Included a score breakdown system to show exactly how scores are calculated

Reflection

Finding a target audience

Our mission for this project was to help combat the spread of misinformation online. This being a grave issue that we, and a majority of our testers and surveyees felt serious about due to its immeasurable impact on our own, as well as others’ wellbeing. With Smart Read, we wanted to equip our users with an easier way to fact check and think critically of the information they are consuming. However, when doing our user research, most of the participants were around the downtown Toronto area and within circles of academia. Our user research is based on this demographic of young, educated adults living in urban Canada. Is this the population that is most susceptible to misinformation?

I believe we should have done preliminary research to see what demographic is most susceptible to misinformation, what kinds of misinformation is prone to being misconstrued, and what audience is likely to accept Smart Read as an intervention. Targeting the demographic that encompasses all 3 of these parameters could really help us maximize Smart Read’s mission and design Smart Read for the people that truly need it the most.

Going Forward

Sharing positivity

There is ample space within Smart Read to introduce positive emotions. Going forward with this project, I would love for the Credibility Score page to have positive animations paired with a good credibility score, and less positive but still encouraging animations coupled with poor scores. Injecting these fun graphics or animations could really boost the experience of an application and also encourage behaviours as well.

I’d also like to add a "share" option, so users can share credibility scores at the same time they’d share a news article. This may be of interest to different audiences and could also help with app outreach, exposure, and encourage others to use Smart Read as well - something that we are missing in the current iteration of the app.
